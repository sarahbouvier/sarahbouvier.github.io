About Me

Hi! My name is Sarah Bouvier, and I am currently a fourth-year student at Northeastern University studying Business Administration with a concentration in Finance and a minor in Data Science. My time at Northeastern has been shaped by meaningful academic experiences, leadership roles, and professional opportunities that have helped me grow both personally and professionally.

One of my most formative early experiences was participating in NUin Berlin during my freshman year, where I got to live and study abroad while exploring 10 countries across Europe. It was an incredible way to start my college journey and gave me a global perspective I carry with me today.

On campus, I am an active member of Delta Sigma Pi, a professional business fraternity, where I have served in two leadership roles: VP of Scholarship and Awards and VP of Chapter Operations. I am also a member of Husky Ambassadors, where I work as both a tour guide and at the registration desk, representing Northeastern to prospective students and their families.

Professionally, I have had the opportunity to gain experience across a range of industries. I spent several years working at a plant nursery in my hometown, starting as a cashier throughout high school and later returning after my freshman year to work in back-end operations, focusing on inventory maintenance. For my first co-op, I worked in Financial Planning and Analysis at Alnylam Pharmaceuticals, where I developed my analytical and finance skills in a fast-paced biotech environment. Most recently, I completed my second co-op on the deal team at Boston Millennia Partners, a life sciences venture capital firm, where I gained hands-on experience evaluating investment opportunities in the life sciences space.
